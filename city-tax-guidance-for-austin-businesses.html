Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between tax planning and tax preparation?

Tax planning involves strategizing to minimize tax liabilities and maximize savings, while tax preparation focuses on accurately completing and filing tax returns. CPAs often offer both services to help individuals and businesses navigate the complexities of the tax system.

Can a CPA assist with financial planning beyond taxes?

Yes, CPAs can provide comprehensive financial planning beyond taxes. They can offer advice on budgeting, investments, retirement planning, and other financial aspects to help clients achieve their long-term financial goals.

Is it necessary to hire a CPA for tax planning if I already have a bookkeeper?

While a bookkeeper can handle day-to-day financial transactions, a CPA brings expertise in tax planning, compliance, and overall financial strategy. Hiring a CPA ensures that your business receives comprehensive financial guidance, especially when facing complex tax issues.
